Privacy Policy for Letter Blast
Effective Date: May 14, 2026
Last Updated: May 17, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Letter Blast ("the App", "we", "us", or "our") handles information when you use the App. Letter Blast is developed and distributed by Umar Muzaffar ("we").

We respect your privacy. Most gameplay progress is stored on your device. We do not operate our own servers to store your personal profile or gameplay history. To improve the App and fix crashes, we use Google Firebase Analytics and Firebase Crashlytics, which may collect limited technical and usage information as described in Section 3. We do not sell your data or use it for third-party advertising.

1. Information We Do NOT Collect Directly
Letter Blast does not ask you to create an account, and we do not collect from you directly:

Your name, email address, phone number, postal address, or any other contact details (unless you email us for support).
Your contacts, photos, microphone, camera, calendar, health data, or any other sensitive system data.
Browsing history, search history, or activity in other apps.
Any data for advertising, cross-app tracking, or behavioral profiling.
We do not use advertising networks, social login, or tracking services for ads. There are no advertisements in the App.

Note: Google Firebase (Section 3) may automatically collect certain technical and usage information when you use the App. That is not listed above because it is processed by Google as our service provider, not collected by us in the same way as account registration data.

2. Information Stored On Your Device
The following information is created and stored only on your device using the iOS system feature UserDefaults. It is used for App functionality on your device and is not sent to our own servers. It is separate from the Firebase data described in Section 3:

A display name of your choice (default value: "Player") used to label scores in the in-app local leaderboard.
Gameplay progress, including your highest score, list of words you have already solved, leaderboard entries with timestamps, remaining arcade runs, and daily-challenge attempts for the current day.
In-app purchase status — flags indicating which difficulty packs you have unlocked and whether you have an active "Unlimited Turns" subscription.
Settings, including your selected age bracket (7–10 or 11+) and your audio mute preference.
If you delete the App from your device, all of this on-device information is erased.

3. Apple Services and Google Firebase
Apple (In-App Purchases)
Letter Blast uses Apple StoreKit to offer in-app purchases and auto-renewable subscriptions. When you make a purchase:

The transaction is processed by Apple, not by us.
Apple may collect billing and account information for the purpose of processing your purchase, in accordance with Apple's own Privacy Policy (https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/).
We receive only what Apple provides to confirm that a purchase is valid for your Apple ID (for example, entitlement to a product). We do not receive your payment card details or billing address.
We do not use Game Center, iCloud sync for gameplay, or push notifications for marketing.

Google Firebase (Analytics and Crashlytics)
We use Google Firebase, provided by Google LLC, as a third-party service provider. We do not control Google's servers. Firebase is used for:

Firebase Analytics — to understand how the App is used (for example, when a game starts or ends, game mode, difficulty, win or loss, and scores sent as event parameters). This helps us improve features and gameplay.
Firebase Crashlytics — to receive crash and stability reports (for example, device model, operating system version, app version, and diagnostic logs) so we can fix bugs.
Depending on your device and settings, Firebase may process information such as:

Firebase app instance or installation identifiers
App interaction and analytics events (as described above)
Crash stack traces and performance-related diagnostic data
General device and app information (for example, device model, OS version, language or locale)
Network-related information processed by Google (Google's systems may derive coarse location from IP address)
We do not use Firebase for advertising or to sell your personal information.

Google's Privacy Policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy
Firebase privacy information: https://firebase.google.com/support/privacy

Your choices: You can review Apple privacy settings on your device (for example, Settings → Privacy & Security). Deleting the App stops new analytics and crash reports from that installation going forward. For questions about Firebase data in relation to Letter Blast, contact us at the email in Section 10.

4. In-App Purchases
The App offers the following purchase types via Apple:

One-time unlocks to access additional difficulty levels.
Consumable packs of in-game "turns".
Auto-renewable subscriptions for unlimited turns (Weekly, Monthly, Yearly).
All payments, refunds, restoration of purchases, and subscription management (including cancellation) are handled by Apple via your Apple ID. You can manage and cancel subscriptions at any time via:

iPhone or iPad → Settings → [Your Apple ID] → Subscriptions.

We do not store your payment information at any time.

5. Children's Privacy
Letter Blast is rated for all ages and may be used by children under the age of 13. We take children's privacy seriously and aim to comply with the U.S. Children's Online Privacy Protection Act ("COPPA") and equivalent laws.

We do not knowingly collect contact information (such as name or email) from children through the App itself.
Gameplay data described in Section 2 is stored only on the device and is used solely for App functionality on that device.
The App may send usage and crash-related information to Google Firebase as described in Section 3. We use that data to improve and stabilize the App, not for advertising.
The App contains a parental gate (a simple arithmetic challenge that young children cannot easily complete) that must be passed before any in-app purchase prompt is shown for the "ages 7–10" gameplay bracket.
The App does not include third-party advertising, behavioral targeting, contests, social features, sharing to social networks, or chat features intended for or directed at children.
If you are a parent or guardian and have questions or concerns, please contact us at the email address in Section 10.

6. How We Use Information
On your device (Section 2), we use information only to:

Show your progress, scores, and leaderboard inside the App.
Track which difficulty levels and daily-challenge attempts you have completed today.
Remember which in-app purchases you have made so we do not ask you to buy the same item twice.
Remember your audio and age-bracket preferences across launches.
We do not transmit this on-device information to our own servers.

Through Google Firebase (Section 3), we use information to:

Understand how players use the App (Analytics).
Diagnose crashes and improve stability (Crashlytics).
We do not use your information for third-party advertising.

7. Information Sharing
We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information.

We share limited technical and usage information with Google LLC (Firebase) solely to provide analytics and crash reporting for the App, as described in Section 3. Google acts as a service provider for those purposes, not for advertising on our behalf.

Purchase validation is handled through Apple as described in Sections 3 and 4.

We may disclose information if required by law, valid legal process, or to protect the rights, property, or safety of users or the public.

8. Data Security and Retention
On-device data is held in standard iOS UserDefaults on your device and is protected by the security of your device and your Apple ID. You can erase all on-device data at any time by deleting the App.

Firebase data is transmitted to Google using industry-standard protections. How long Google retains that data is governed by Google's policies and our Firebase project settings. We do not operate our own database of your gameplay profile.

9. Your Rights
Depending on where you live (including the EU/UK under GDPR and California under CCPA/CPRA), you may have rights regarding personal information, such as access, deletion, or restriction of certain processing.

Access / Portability (on-device): Your gameplay progress is visible inside the App. It is stored on your device as described in Section 2.
Deletion (on-device): Delete the App to erase local data, or reinstall to reset progress.
Firebase / Google: Data processed by Google is subject to Google's privacy policy and any choices Google may offer. See Section 3.
Questions: Contact us using the email in Section 10. We will respond within a reasonable time.
The App does not use advertising or cross-app tracking. Firebase Analytics is used for product analytics, not for ads.
